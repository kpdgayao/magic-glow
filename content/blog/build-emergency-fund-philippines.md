# Where to Build Your Emergency Fund in the Philippines

An emergency fund is the foundation of financial security. Before investing, before splurging, before anything ‚Äî you need money set aside for life's surprises.

## What Is an Emergency Fund?

It's cash you can access quickly when unexpected expenses hit:

- Medical emergencies
- Phone or laptop repair/replacement
- Job loss or income gap
- Family emergencies
- Unexpected travel

## How Much Do You Need?

The standard advice is 3-6 months of living expenses. For creators with irregular income, aim for the higher end:

| Monthly Expenses | Target (6 months) |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| ‚Ç±5,000 | ‚Ç±30,000 |
| ‚Ç±10,000 | ‚Ç±60,000 |
| ‚Ç±15,000 | ‚Ç±90,000 |
| ‚Ç±20,000 | ‚Ç±120,000 |

**Start with ‚Ç±10,000.** That's your first milestone ‚Äî enough to handle most minor emergencies. Then keep building.

## Where to Keep Your Emergency Fund

Your emergency fund needs to be:

1. **Safe** ‚Äî No risk of losing money
2. **Liquid** ‚Äî Accessible within 24-48 hours
3. **Earning interest** ‚Äî Even a little helps fight inflation

## The Best Strategy: Split It

Don't put all your eggs in one basket:

1. **‚Ç±5,000 in GCash/Maya** ‚Äî Immediate access for urgent needs
2. **Bulk in a digital bank** (Tonik, SeaBank, GoTyme) ‚Äî Earns higher interest
3. **Optional: ‚Ç±20,000+ in a traditional bank** ‚Äî Backup access via ATM/branch

## How to Build Your Emergency Fund

### The ‚Ç±100/Day Method

Save ‚Ç±100 every day. In 30 days, you have ‚Ç±3,000. In 100 days, ‚Ç±10,000. It sounds small but it adds up surprisingly fast.

### The Percentage Method

Set aside 20% of every payment you receive ‚Äî before spending anything. If you earn ‚Ç±10,000 from a brand deal, ‚Ç±2,000 goes straight to your emergency fund.

### The Windfall Rule

Got an unexpectedly large payment? Put at least 50% into your emergency fund until you hit your target.

## Rules for Your Emergency Fund

1. **Only use it for real emergencies** ‚Äî A sale at Shopee is not an emergency
2. **Replenish immediately** ‚Äî If you use it, make rebuilding your top priority
3. **Don't invest it** ‚Äî Stocks, crypto, and mutual funds are not emergency funds (they can lose value)
4. **Keep it separate** ‚Äî Use a different account than your spending money

## What About Pag-IBIG MP2?

Pag-IBIG MP2 is great for medium-term savings (5+ years) with historically high dividends (6-7% p.a.), but it's **not ideal for an emergency fund** because:

- Lock-in period of 5 years
- Withdrawal takes time
- Better suited for a separate savings goal

Build your emergency fund first, then start MP2 for long-term growth.
